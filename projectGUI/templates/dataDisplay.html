Dataset Overview

The dataset contains the following two levels of data which distinguishes it from most of the existing open-sourced solar forecasting datasets and makes it especially suitable for deep-learning-based solar forecasting research:

  • Benchmark dataset: 3 years of processed sky images (64×64) and concurrent PV power generation data with 1-min interval that are ready-to-use for deep learning model development
  • Raw dataset: Overlapping high resolution sky video footage (2048×2048) recorded at 20 frames per second, sky image frames (2048×2048) and history PV power generation data logged in 1-min frequency that suit various research purposes

Sky Images Collection

About the Sky Images
Our daytime sky footage (6:00 AM to 8:00 PM PST) is captured by a high-resolution 360° fisheye camera mounted atop Stanford's Green Earth Sciences Building, pointing 14° south by west. The camera records crisp 2048×2048 video at 20 frames per second, with still images saved every minute.

Flexible Use
Need different settings? Extract images at higher frequencies or resize them (e.g., 64×64 works well for solar forecasting). Camera settings like aperture and white balance stay fixed for consistency.
